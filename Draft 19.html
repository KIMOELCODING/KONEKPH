Indonesia to Host ASEAN Climate Summit 2025

Rina Wulandari Politics June 24, 2025

Jakarta, Indonesia –

Indonesia has officially been selected as the host country for the ASEAN Climate Summit 2025, marking a historic milestone in the nation's diplomatic and environmental efforts. This will be the first time a Southeast Asian country leads the summit with a full green energy and sustainability agenda.

The announcement was made during the ASEAN Ministerial Meeting held in Kuala Lumpur last weekend. Indonesia's proposal stood out for its ambitious commitment to renewable energy, climate resilience programs, and sustainable urban development.

A Regional Focus on Climate Action

The summit is expected to bring together leaders from all 10 ASEAN member states, along with representatives from international organizations and environmental groups. Key topics on the agenda include carbon reduction targets, green financing, and cross-border environmental cooperation.

Indonesia's Minister of Environment and Forestry stated that the country aims to use the summit as a platform to showcase its national progress in reducing deforestation and transitioning to renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and geothermal power.

Implications for the Real Estate Sector

Industry analysts note that hosting such a high-profile summit will have downstream effects on Indonesia's real estate market, particularly in Jakarta and surrounding areas. Increased foreign interest and infrastructure development are expected to drive demand for commercial and mixed-use properties in the capital region.
